Getting Started

What is UEFN?

UEFN stands for Unreal Editor for Fortnite, which is a powerful toolset that allows creators to build and publish a wide variety of games and experiences directly within the Fortnite ecosystem. It uses the power of Unreal Engine to bring advanced features to creators.

What is Verse?

Verse is a programming language developed by Epic Games specifically for the metaverse. It's used in UEFN to create custom game logic, mechanics, and interactive elements that go beyond the capabilities of devices and pre-built templates.

How do I get help with a project?

Common Issues

My custom car mesh isn't showing. Is it fixable?

Yes, but your car mesh is a different size from the model I used in the video. You must set the size of the mesh to 1 on all axes and the rotations on all axes to 0. If you still can't see the car, make the mesh bigger. If not, follow the tutorial again.

My custom car mesh doesnt move. Fix possible?

Please make sure you are using the template i provided in the post and did the steps correctly. if the issue persists please either contact me or make a ticket in the server

My custom car doesn't have its normal color. How can I fix it?

You have to go to the material (or make a new one) **not the material instance**. On the left side, search for "Niagara" and tick the 3 boxes that pop up.

My pickaxe is in the ground. How can I fix it?

In the VFX Powerup Device, search for "Socket" and change it to **RIGHT HAND**.

My pickaxe doesn't have its normal color. How can I fix it?

You have to go to the material (or make a new one) **not the material instance**. On the left side, search for "Niagara" and tick the 3 boxes that pop up.

My pickaxe isn't visible. How can I fix it?

Go to the Niagra System and click on Mesh Renderer. In Mesh, click it and locate Index [0] and add your static mesh of the pickaxe.

My pickaxe looks weird (as of rotation). Fixable?

Pickaxes will always need testing and adjusting until you get your rotation just how you like it. For most cases, you only need to make the Z-AXIS to "90".
